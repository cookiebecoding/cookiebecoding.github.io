Historical Beer Nations are Drinking Less Beer

... but more wine. The chart below shows the evolution of beer consumption for 5 traditionally 'beer drinking countries': Germany, Australia, New Zealand, Belgium and Ireland. In the 2nd half of the 2Oth century, no other country in the world was consuming as much beer as these five.
Today, this consumption has noticeably decreased, and for certain countries even halved. Meanwhile, people in these beer countries are consuming more wine - with exception for Germany. (Scroll down to check out the wine countries.)

Wine Nations as well are Drinking Less Wine

... but still stick to wine. Similarly to the trend we see for beer drinking in beer countries, the wine consumption in historical 'wine drinking countries' has been decreasing as well. However, with exception of Spain - where since the early 2000's beer takes a more important part in the alcohol consumption than wine - for most wine countries the consumption of beer increases only a little or not at all.
